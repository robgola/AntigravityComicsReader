Introduction

ACR (Antigravity Comic Reader) is a specialized client designed to browse, manage, and read your comic book collection hosted on a Komga Server.

It provides a seamless experience for browsing libraries, exploring detailed series information, and reading comics with an optimized, immersive reader.

Chapter 1: Configuration

Before you begin, you must connect the application to your Komga server.

1. Navigate to the Settings tab in the top menu.
2. You will see the Server Configuration panel. Currently, this displays the connected server:
  • Server Name: The friendly name of your server.
  • Address: The URL/IP of your Komga instance.
  • Port: The connection port (default 8443).
  • User: The logged-in user.

Note: These settings are currently read-only and configured in the app constants.

Chapter 2: Browsing Libraries

Use the Import tab to browse your server content.

Library View

This is the main entry point. You will see a grid of all available libraries on your server.

  • Tap a Library Card to enter that library.
  • The card displays the library name and a representative cover image.

Series List

Once inside a library, you will browse its Series.

  • Folders vs. Flat List: You can toggle between a folder-based view (directories) and a flat alphabetical list of series using the toggle button in the header.
  • Folder Navigation: Tap on folders to drill down into sub-directories.
  • Series Selection: Tap on a Series card to view its books.

Book List (Series Detail)

This view displays all books within a selected series.

  • Hero Section: The layout features a highlighted "Hero" section at the top displaying the focused book's cover and metadata (Writer, Penciller, Inker, Summary).
  • Selection: Tap any book in the bottom grid to focus it in the Hero section.
  • Multi-Select: Tap the background to deselect. (Selecting multiple books for batch actions is a planned feature).

Chapter 3: Reading

The core experience of ACR is reading your comics.

1. From the Book List, tap the yellow Read button in the Hero section.
2. The book will download (streaming) and open in the Reader.

Reader Controls

  • Turn Pages: Tap the left or right edges of the screen, or swipe.
  • Menu: Tap the center of the screen to toggle the controls (Top Bar and Thumbnail slider).
  • Thumbnails: Use the bottom slider to quickly jump to any page.
  • Close: Tap the "Done" button in the top left to return to the book list.

Chapter 4: Importing for Offline

You can download books to your local device library ("My Library").

1. In the Book List, select a book (or view it in the Hero section).
2. Tap the Import button.
3. Confirm the download. The book will be saved to your device and appear in the Library tab (Local Library).
